Crystal Garden III

Where Crystal Garden II solves the inverse problem (target → rule sequence), this is the forward sweep: drop one seed, run one rule forever, and watch what 3D form grows. The Wolfram question for an FCC universe.

The model

A 13-bit FCC cell in its broadcast projection: each occupied cell radiates a signal to all 12 nearest neighbors. A cell's fate depends only on k = how many of its 12 neighbors are occupied, via a Life-like Birth / Survival rule:

• empty cell → born if k ∈ B
• occupied cell → survives if k ∈ S

One structural law

A lone seed has 12 neighbors at k = 1. So a single seed can only sprout if 1 ∈ B — otherwise it dies or freezes. Every growing rule here contains 1 in B.
